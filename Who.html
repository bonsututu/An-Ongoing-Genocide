About the Uyghurs

   

Background

 

- The Uyghurs have a large population in the Xinjiang Region of northwest China with some of

the population also residing in Uzebekistan, Khazakstan, and Kyrgyzstan.

 

- The Uyghurs are amongst the older Turkic-speaking peoples of Central Asia.

 

- Popular Uyghur cities are: Ürümqi, Xinjiang (Capital City), Kahgar.

 

- The Uyghur people of Xinjiang practice Islam. Specifically, they are Sunni Muslims.

 

- Many Ugyhurs work in petroleum extraction, mining, and manufacturing in urban centers.

 

- The Uyghurs are known to be sedentary village-dwelling people who reside in valleys of mountain systems.

 

- Principal foods: Wheat, Corn, Kaoliang, and Melons.

 

- The industrial crop of the Uyghur regions of China is cotton, which has been grown for a long time in mass production.

   

Language

 

- The Uyghur language was originally written in Arabic script but then modified by the Soviet Union in 1947.

But then the Arabic script returned in 1983.

 

- The modern version of the Uyghur language is based on the Taranchi dialect that is spoken in Russia.

 

- Xinjiang University taught classes in Uyghur and Mandarin but were ordered to stop teaching in Uyghur in 2002.
