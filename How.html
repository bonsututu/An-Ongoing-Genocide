How Did It Come To This?

   

- During the 1950s, another Chinese ethnic group names the Han began moving into the Xinjiang region in large numbers.

That number continued to grow largely decade after decade.

 

- By the time it was the late 20th century (1990s), the Han had populated 2/5 of the Xinjiang region's population.

 

- As time went by and the Han population began to grow, economic disputes and ethnic tensions began to emerge.

 

- Resulting from the tensions and disputes came many protests and riots.

 

- One riot that stood out was in July of 2009, where about 200 people were killed and 1,700 were injured.

 

- Violent attacks like stabbings and suicide bombings continued to occur frequently.

   

- Chinese authorities began to respond to these violent attacks by suspecting Uyghurs as separatists and dissidents.

 

- Cameras and checkpoints were put in place in areas of Xinjiang with dense Uyghur populations.

 

- The CCP (Chinese Communist Party) also began incarcerating Uyghurs without trial and placing Uyghurs into internment camps where Uyghur relgious practices are being surpressed.
